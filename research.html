Geometry Field Splatting with Gaussian Surfels

CVPR 2025
Introduces a method for precise geometric reconstruction of opaque surfaces from images by adapting Gaussian surfels to splat geometry fields. Derives an efficient, nearly exact differentiable rendering algorithm for geometry fields parameterized by Gaussian surfels, eliminating common approximations. Ensures continuity in rendered color despite surfel clustering and demonstrates significant improvements in 3D surface reconstruction quality on standard benchmarks.
Kaiwen Jiang / Venkataram Sivaram / Cheng Peng / Ravi Ramamoorthi

Neural Geometry Fields for Meshes

SIGGRAPH 2024 (Conference)
Introduces a novel neural representation for 3D mesh surfaces by modeling geometry with coarse quadrangular patches and refining details using coordinate neural networks. Represents discrete surface geometry as a continuous field, enabling extraction of traditional triangular meshes by sampling neural displacements. Excels at mesh compression, significantly reducing memory usage without compromising surface detail, and broadens the applicability of neural surface representations to discrete mesh data.
Venkataram Sivaram / Tzu-Mao Li / Ravi Ramamoorthi

Parameter-space ReSTIR for Differentiable and Inverse Rendering

SIGGRAPH 2023 (Conference)
Develops an algorithm to efficiently reuse Monte Carlo gradient samples between gradient descent iterations in differentiable rendering, inspired by reservoir-based temporal importance resampling. Reformulates differential rendering integrals in parameter space and introduces a new estimator for handling negative functions, enabling significant reduction in gradient error and faster convergence for inverse rendering tasks involving complex lighting and textures.
Wesley Chang / Venkataram Sivaram / Derek Nowrouzezahrai / Toshiya Hachisuka / Ravi Ramamoorthi / Tzu-Mao Li
